# Geographic & Regional Performance – Business Recommendations

## 1. Strategic Overview

This document translates the World Bank indicator analysis into actionable recommendations for **market prioritization**, **regional expansion**, and **capital allocation**. The framework supports strategic planning for multinational banking—relevant to ING's operations across 40+ countries.

---

## 2. Market Attractiveness Framework

| Factor | Weight | Data Source | Use |
|--------|--------|-------------|-----|
| **GDP per capita** | 30% | World Bank | Purchasing power, market size |
| **GDP growth (YoY)** | 30% | World Bank | Momentum, opportunity |
| **Urbanization** | 20% | World Bank | Digital/retail banking potential |
| **Stability/risk** | 20% | External | Political, regulatory risk |

**Composite score** = weighted sum; rank markets for expansion and resource allocation.

---

## 3. Regional Priorities

| Region / Market | Observation | Recommended Action |
|-----------------|-------------|---------------------|
| **China, India** | Large GDP, high growth, urbanizing | Prioritize digital, retail expansion; monitor regulatory |
| **USA, Japan, EU** | High GDP per capita, mature | Focus on efficiency, innovation, cross-sell |
| **Brazil** | Large economy, high urbanization | Balance growth opportunity with macro risk |
| **Netherlands** | Home market; stable | Core operations; regulatory alignment |

## 4. Operational Workflow

| Step | Owner | Action |
|------|-------|--------|
| 1. Update indicators | Strategy / Analytics | Run script quarterly; refresh World Bank data |
| 2. Compute composite score | Strategy | Apply weights; rank markets |
| 3. Review with regional teams | Strategy / Business | Validate assumptions; adjust priorities |
| 4. Allocate resources | Finance / Business | Align budget and headcount with rankings |
| 5. Monitor and report | Strategy | Track score changes; escalate material shifts |
